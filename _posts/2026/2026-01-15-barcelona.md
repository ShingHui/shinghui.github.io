  <p>Fueled by pastries, I took the Metro (L4) to Passeig de Gràcia and switched to the <strong>R1 Train</strong> towards Blanes. The train runs right along the coast—a scenic 1.5-hour ride.</p>

  <div class="info-box warning">
    <strong>⚠️ Important Transport Alert:</strong><br>
    I originally thought my <strong>48h City Pass</strong> covered the trip to Blanes, but I was wrong! 
    <br><br>
    <strong>The Issue:</strong> The City Pass is usually for Zone 1, but Blanes is in <strong>Zone 5</strong>. I actually had trouble exiting the gates at Blanes station because my ticket wasn't valid there.
    <br><br>
    <strong>Solution:</strong> Don't make my mistake! Please buy a separate <strong>Rodalies ticket (Zone 5)</strong> specifically for Blanes at the station machine. It costs around €6-7 one way.
  </div>

  <p><strong>Hiking the Coastline (1:20 PM):</strong><br>
  Since the <em>Jardí Botànic Marimurtra</em> closed early at 3 PM, I pivoted to a coastal hike. The sea breeze and the winter sun created a warmth I hadn't felt in weeks.</p>
  
  <ul>
    <li><strong>Castell de Sant Joan:</strong> A bit of a climb, but the 360-degree vistas of the Mediterranean were breathtaking.</li>
    <li><strong>Sa Palomera Rock:</strong> The symbolic beginning of the Costa Brava.</li>
    <li><strong>Cala de Sant Francesc:</strong> A 20-minute hike past the gardens led to this hidden gem.</li>
  </ul>

  <p>Ended the day with churros at <strong>Xurreria Trebol (€4.95)</strong> and a communal dinner back at Yeah Hostel.</p>

  <div class="section-day">
    <span>Feb 16</span>
    Gaudí’s Dream & Cocktail Trains
  </div>

  <p><strong>Morning Vibes:</strong> Strolled through the Gothic Quarter (Barri Gòtic) and found the famous "Kiss of Freedom" mural. Grabbed brunch at <em>Lato Cafe</em> (€22.50) before heading to the main event.</p>

  <div class="spot-highlight">
    <img src="/assets/img/2026/barcelona/sagrada.jpg" alt="Sagrada Familia" class="spot-img">
    <div class="spot-text">
      <span class="spot-meta">The Masterpiece</span>
      <h3>Sagrada Familia</h3>
      <p>If you think the outside is unbelievable, wait until you step inside. It is simply gorgeous. When you see the details, you realize why it has taken over 100 years to build.</p>
      <p>For architecture lovers, this is Mecca. Even if you aren't religious, it is a must-see.</p>
      <p><em>Student Ticket: €24.00</em></p>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="info-box tip">
    <strong>☀️ Golden Hour Tip:</strong><br>
    Book your slot for <strong>after 3:00 PM</strong>. This is when the sun hits the stained glass windows, flooding the interior with incredible colors.
  </div>

  <p>After soaking in the art, I grabbed a drink at <em>Chinese Tofu Magician</em> (€6.80) and headed to San Sebastian Beach for some guitar vibes and sunset.</p>

  <div class="spot-highlight reverse">
    <img src="/assets/img/2026/barcelona/bar.jpeg" alt="Bar Orient Express" class="spot-img">
    <div class="spot-text">
      <span class="spot-meta">Nightlife Experience</span>
      <h3>Bar Orient Express</h3>
      <p>A unique experience! You play a game on a vintage phone to pick your cocktail, and then the drink arrives <strong>on a model train</strong> that stops right in front of you.</p>
      <p>The waiter was fully in character, making the service 10/10. Highly recommended for a fun night out.</p>
      <p><em>Cost: €18.00</em></p>
    </div>

      <h3>The Cappuccino Incident</h3>
      <p>I ordered the <em>Pasta con le Sarde</em> (Sardines) and... a Cappuccino.</p>
      <p><strong>OMG.</strong> The waiter gave me <em>the look</em>. I know it's an Italian sin to drink milk coffee after noon, but I barely slept at the airport and needed the caffeine!</p>
      <p>Embarrassment aside, the pasta was lovingly prepared and awesome. I tried to come back on a second trip with family, but it was sold out.</p>
      <p><em>Total: €17.30 (Make a reservation! They turn people away daily.)</em></p>
