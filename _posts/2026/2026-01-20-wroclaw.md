    <h1>Wroclaw: Dwarfs, Gothic Spires & Hidden Gems</h1>

  </div>

  <div class="intro-box">
    "I fell in love with this charming town instantly. From the calm hues of the Market Square to the nearly 1,000 gnomes hiding in the streets. I visited twice—once for the sightseeing, and once just for the Korean BBQ. Here is my complete list of things to do."
  </div>

  <div class="spot-card">
      <span class="spot-name">🏰 Arrival: The Yellow Castle</span>
      I have to mention the arrival experience. <strong>Wrocław Główny</strong> isn't just a train station; it looks like a yellow castle straight out of a Disney movie. Don't rush out—take a moment to admire the architecture inside and out. It’s one of the most beautiful stations in Poland.
  </div>

  <div class="guide-section">
    <div class="section-title">
      <span class="section-number">1</span>
      The Colorful Market Square
    </div>
    <p>I would say this is the prettiest Old Town market square I’ve ever seen in Poland. The colorful buildings mix Gothic structures with Renaissance elements. The locals said it took years to restore after WWII, and the result looks like a fairy wonderland.</p>
    <div class="spot-card">
      <span class="spot-name">💡 Why visit?</span>
      For Asian travelers, this architecture is completely unique. It feels different and new. Just wander around and soak in the colors.
    </div>
    <div class="spot-card">
      <span class="spot-name">🎄 Christmas Market</span>
      Since I visited in winter, the Market Square was transformed into a massive Christmas Market. It’s widely considered the best in Poland, full of lights, mulled wine (Grzaniec), and cozy vibes. If you visit in December, prepare for crowds but a magical atmosphere.
    </div>

      <span class="section-number">2</span>
      Hunting Games: The City of Gnomes
    </div>
    <p>Why has this Polish city been invaded by dwarfs? There's a story behind it.</p>
    <div class="history-quote">
       It started in the 1980s with the <strong>Orange Alternative</strong>, a student movement using humor to mock the authoritarian regime. They painted dwarfs over censored graffiti. Today, these statues honor that resistance and have become a beloved city-wide scavenger hunt.
    </div>
    <p>There are nearly 1,000 of them! Keep your eyes on the ground.</p>

    <div class="section-title">
      <span class="section-number">3</span>
      The Foodie Detour: Asian Comfort Food
    </div>
    <p>If you've never tried Polish cuisine, definitely try it first. But as an exchange student, sometimes I just crave the taste of home. Surprisingly, Wroclaw has amazing Asian restaurants.</p>

    <div class="spot-card food-card">
      <span class="food-badge">Korean BBQ</span>
      <span class="spot-name">Galbi restauracja koreańska & grill</span>
      <p>My friend and I took a 6-hour train trip round-trip just for this. Totally crazy, but totally worth it.</p>
      <ul>
        <li><strong>Cost:</strong> ~96 zł per person (Unlimited sides, kimchi jjigae, drinks).</li>
        <li><strong>The Rule:</strong> You must order two portions of the same meat for the grill (a bit weird, but the food makes up for it).</li>
        <li><strong>Verdict:</strong> Great food, fresh, helpful staff. A bit far from the center, but perfect for hanging out with friends.</li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div class="spot-card food-card">
      <span class="food-badge">Malatang</span>
      <span class="spot-name">Pan Pot Mala (Real Chinese Flavor)</span>
      <p>If you are a spice lover, go here! This is self-service (build-your-own bowl) with ingredients like shrimp, squid, mushrooms, and noodles. Payment is by weight.</p>
      <div class="info-tag">💰 70-100 zł/portion</div>
      <div class="info-tag">🌶️ Real Chinese Spice Level</div>
    </div>

    <div class="spot-card food-card">

      <span class="spot-name">DingTea Wroclaw</span>
      <p>Studying in Europe makes you crave bubble tea. This place is run by friendly Vietnamese staff with ingredients imported from Taiwan. The Brown Sugar flavor is the best.</p>
      <div class="info-tag">🧋 20-30 zł/drink</div>

    <div class="spot-card">
      <span class="spot-name">⛪ Ostrów Tumski (Cathedral Island)</span>
      <p>The "Gem of Wroclaw" This is where the city began. It’s incredibly peaceful and filled with religious buildings.</p>
      <ul>
         <li><strong>Most Piaskowy (Sand Bridge):</strong> The oldest bridge (1861).</li>
         <li><strong>Most Tumski (Tumski Bridge):</strong> The famous "Lovers Bridge" with padlocks (though many were removed recently!).</li>
         <li><strong>The Lamplighter:</strong> At dusk, look for the man lighting the gas lamps by hand (we missed him, but maybe you will see him!).</li>
         <li><strong>Botanical Gardens:</strong> A peaceful escape nearby.</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
