<h1 style="margin-top: 0; padding-top: 10px;">Inspiration</h1>

<p class="lead" style="font-style: italic; color: #666;">Where creativity meets perception</p>

<div style="display: flex; flex-wrap: wrap; gap: 40px; margin-bottom: 50px;">

  <div style="flex: 2; min-width: 300px;">
    <p>The project draws inspiration from ‚ÄúTainan 400, Here We Meet‚Äù, a celebration of Tainan‚Äôs four centuries of cultural and historical fusion.</p>
    
    <p>I was inspired by how the Beuchet Chair Illusion challenges perception ‚Äî just as Tainan‚Äôs identity intertwines past and present, illusion and reality.</p>
    
    <p>Since the project required an interactive and visually striking design that could leave a strong impression on viewers, our instructor encouraged us to explore perceptual experiences.</p>
    
    <p>After studying various interactive artworks, I decided to reinterpret the concept of illusion through the traditional ‚ÄúChair 400.‚Äù</p>
  </div>
  
<div style="flex: 1; min-width: 250px; background-color: #fff5f5; padding: 25px; border-radius: 12px; border-left: 5px solid #ff9999; height: fit-content;">
    <p style="font-family: serif; font-size: 1.1em; color: #cc4444; margin: 0;">
      "The design incorporates symbolic elements from Bischofia javanica and Punica granatum"‚Äî two species deeply rooted in Tainan‚Äôs 400-year heritage ‚Äî combined with local wood inlay and carving craftsmanship to express the city‚Äôs enduring cultural essence.
    </p>

<h1 style="margin-top: 0; padding-top: 10px;">Research Foundation</h1>

<p class="lead" style="font-style: italic; color: #666;">To better understand the mechanism behind optical illusions, my research focused on three key references:</p>

<div style="display: flex; flex-wrap: wrap; gap: 20px;">
  
  <div style="flex: 1; min-width: 220px; background: var(--card-bg); border: 1px solid var(--card-border-color); padding: 25px; border-radius: 15px; box-shadow: 0 4px 10px rgba(0,0,0,0.03);">
    <i class="fas fa-eye" style="color: #a07e7eff; font-size: 28px; margin-bottom: 15px; display: block;"></i>
    <h3 style="font-size: 1.1rem;">‚ÄúYour Amazing Brain‚Äù Exhibition (2022) </h3>
    <p style="font-size: 0.9em; opacity: 0.8;">An interactive neuroscience exhibition developed by the Wellcome Centre for Integrative Neuroimaging at the University of Oxford and Banbury Museum & Gallery. It explored perceptual and optical illusions to demonstrate how the brain interprets scale, distance, and visual cues. </p>
  </div>

  <div style="flex: 1; min-width: 220px; background: var(--card-bg); border: 1px solid var(--card-border-color); padding: 25px; border-radius: 15px; box-shadow: 0 4px 10px rgba(0,0,0,0.03);">
    <i class="fas fa-book-open" style="color: #a07e7eff; font-size: 28px; margin-bottom: 15px; display: block;"></i>
    <h3 style="font-size: 1.1rem;">Jean Beuchet (1963) </h3>
    <p style="font-size: 0.9em; opacity: 0.8;">The original study that introduced the Beuchet Chair illusion, analyzing how perceived distance affects apparent size. This research established the foundational principle behind the illusion used in this project.</p>
  </div>

  <div style="flex: 1; min-width: 220px; background: var(--card-bg); border: 1px solid var(--card-border-color); padding: 25px; border-radius: 15px; box-shadow: 0 4px 10px rgba(0,0,0,0.03);">
    <i class="fas fa-pen" style="color: #a07e7eff; font-size: 28px; margin-bottom: 15px; display: block;"></i>
    <h3 style="font-size: 1.1rem;">Wiseman, R. (2016) </h3>
    <p style="font-size: 0.9em; opacity: 0.8;">A contemporary reinterpretation of the Beuchet Chair illusion that refined viewing conditions and structural composition.  This article inspiring me to explore how the same perceptual principle could be adapted into a cultural context.</p>
  </div>
</div>

    </h4>
    <ul style="color: #444; line-height: 1.7;">
      <li>
        <strong>Finding the Ratio:</strong> Through analyzing video footage, I discovered that a <strong>1:3 scale</strong> successfully produced the intended illusion. (Future exploration: 1:2 scale or alternative angles).
      </li>
      <li>
        <strong>Structural Simplification:</strong> Refining the structure from <strong>four legs to three</strong> simplified the overall system, eliminating the need for complex chassis calculations.
      </li>
    </ul>
